Know Your Violation

Understanding Your Local Law 152 Violation

There are two main types of violations issued for Local Law 152 non-compliance. Understanding which type you have is the first step toward clearing it.

DOB Violations

Issued directly by the Department of Buildings when a building fails to file a GPS-2 certification by the required deadline. These violations appear on your building's BIS (Building Information System) record and carry civil penalties.

Typical penalty: Up to $10,000 for the first year of non-compliance.

ECB/OATH Violations

Issued through the Environmental Control Board (now OATH — Office of Administrative Trials and Hearings). ECB violations require a formal hearing and can result in escalating penalties. Unresolved ECB violations may lead to liens on your property.

Typical penalty: Varies by hearing outcome; penalties escalate with continued non-compliance.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • First year of non-compliance Up to $10,000
  • Each additional year +$10,000/year
  • ECB hearing penalties Varies (can exceed DOB fines)
  • Unresolved violations Property liens possible

Don't Wait

Why You Should Act Now

Fines Escalate Every Year

Local Law 152 penalties increase by $10,000 for each year of continued non-compliance. The longer you wait, the more you pay. Acting now stops the clock on escalating fines.

Property Liens

Unresolved ECB violations can result in liens placed on your property, affecting your ability to sell, refinance, or transfer ownership. Clearing the violation removes this risk.

Insurance & Liability Exposure

An open gas safety violation can complicate insurance claims and increase liability exposure if a gas-related incident occurs. Compliance protects you legally and financially.

Building Sale & Refinancing

Open DOB violations appear on your building's public record and are flagged during due diligence. Clearing violations before a sale or refinance avoids delays and lost deals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Violation Clearance FAQ

How long does it take to clear a Local Law 152 violation?

The timeline depends on your specific violation and building conditions. Once we complete the inspection and file the GPS-2 certification with the DOB, dismissal typically takes a few weeks to process. We work to resolve violations as quickly as possible.

Can I clear a violation after the deadline has passed?

Yes. Even if your compliance deadline has passed and a violation has been issued, you can still complete the required inspection and filing to clear it. The sooner you act, the lower the total penalties. Contact us immediately to begin the process.

What is the difference between a DOB violation and an ECB violation?

A DOB violation is issued by the Department of Buildings and appears on your building's BIS record. An ECB violation is issued through the Environmental Control Board (now OATH) and typically requires a hearing. ECB violations can result in additional penalties and may lead to property liens if unresolved.

Will the violation appear on my building's public record?

Yes — DOB and ECB violations are part of the public record and appear on the NYC BIS system. They are visible during any title search or due diligence process. Clearing the violation updates your building's record to show compliance.

How much does violation clearance cost?

Costs vary depending on the type of violation, building size, and any corrections needed. Contact us for a free assessment — we will review your violation notice and provide a clear quote before any work begins.
