Local Law 152 Is a Safety Requirement and a Liability Shield for Landlords

For NYC property owners, Local Law 152 is more than a compliance deadline. It is a core risk-management measure that protects tenants, buildings, and ownership.

Why This Matters for Landlords

Gas-related incidents can create severe legal, financial, and reputational exposure. Local Law 152 requires periodic gas piping inspections so issues are found early and corrected before they become emergencies. Ignoring this process can leave landlords vulnerable to DOB enforcement, insurance complications, tenant claims, and avoidable repair costs.

Liability Risk Is Not Just About Fines

Many owners focus only on penalties, but liability extends much further. If an incident occurs, stakeholders will ask whether ownership followed required inspection and reporting obligations. A missing or delayed compliance record can make legal defense harder and increase settlement risk.

  • Documented inspections show proactive safety management.
  • Timely GPS-1 and GPS-2 filings demonstrate regulatory compliance.
  • Correction records support defensible operations when liability is questioned.
  • Consistent inspection cycles reduce long-term operational and financial risk.

What a Strong Compliance Program Looks Like

Effective Local Law 152 management is a repeatable process: schedule inspections based on your community district cycle, complete corrective work quickly, submit required filings on time, and maintain organized documentation across your portfolio.

Bottom Line

Local Law 152 is a safety obligation and a liability control strategy. For landlords, the cost of noncompliance often exceeds the cost of doing inspections correctly and on time.
